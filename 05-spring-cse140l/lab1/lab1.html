CSE 140L Hardware Lab One, Spring 2005, CK Cheng

Combinational Circuit Design:
Hierarchical Ripple Adder and MUX

Due time: Right before class on April 20

Objective

In this lab, we will build a full-adder, a 4-bit adder from the full-adder, and further a 16-bit adder from the 4-bit adder. Likewise, we will hierarchically build a 2-input multiplexer, a 4-input multiplexer and a 16-input multiplexer.

We study the functions and the delay of the logics. Use ONE worst case to demonstrate the delay on the critical path.

Note that in the lab, we use FPGA Family: Virtex2, Device Type: XC2V80-5CS144.

Part 1. Tutorial

Go through the Xilinx ISE 6 In-Depth Tutorial (Chapter 3: Schematic-Based Design, Chapter 5: Design Implementation, Chapter 6: Timing Simulation)

Write a summary commenting on the overall process of using Xilinx and how to avoid the possible mistakes in less than 100 words.

Part 2. Adders

I.

Build a full adder using Xilinx program. Here is tutorial on how to build and simulate a full adder in Xilinx. Simulate the behavior of the logic to verify the function. Use the post-place&route simulation to demonstrate the worst delay of the circuit. Mark the amount of the delay.

II.

Using the full adder built in I, build a 4-bit adder & simulate the 4-bit adder. Verify the function and demonstrate the worst delay of the circuit. Mark the amount of the delay.

III.

Using the 4-bit adder block built in II, build a 16-bit adder & simulate the 16-bit adder. Verify the function and  demonstrate the worst delay of the circuit. Mark the amount of the delay.

Part 3. Multiplexers

I.

Build a circuit that translates a 4-bit parallel data to a serial output using only 2:1 Muxes. Define the specification for this 4-bit parallel to serial circuit. (input, output & their relationship)

II.

Build & simulate the circuit. Verify the function and demonstrate the worst delay of the circuit. Mark the amount of the delay.

III.

Using the 4:1 Mux block built in III, build & simulate a 16:1 Mux. Verify the function and demonstrate the worst delay of the circuit. Mark the amount of the delay. If you can't find a test bench to demonstrate the worst delay, explain why.
